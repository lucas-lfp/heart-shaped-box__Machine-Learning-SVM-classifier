Cardiovascular disease stands as the foremost cause of death globally, accounting for approximately a third of all fatalities 1.

The spectrum of cardiovascular diseases encompasses conditions like Ischemic Heart Disease, Stroke, and Heart Failure, among others. Notably, Ischemic Heart Disease, Stroke, and Hypertensive Heart Disease are the most burdensome 2.

Risk factors for cardiovascular diseases are varied and include high cholesterol, hypertension, tobacco use, and diabetes, among others. It's important to note that not all cardiovascular diseases share the same risk factors. Moreover, numerous individual factors also play a role, including gender, stress, genetics, family history, environment, concurrent diseases, and medication use 3.

Given the uniqueness of each individual, Data Science proves invaluable. This project aimed to develop a machine learning model to assess an individual's risk of cardiovascular disease based on specific information: age, weight, height, blood pressure, cholesterol, and glucose levels. Medical practice often employs simple algorithms and scores for diagnosis or prognosis, but machine learning offers a robust approach to consider a broad set of individual characteristics and their interrelations.

This model employs a Support Vector Machine (SVM) and was trained on data from 65,000 individuals, both with and without cardiovascular disease, achieving an accuracy of 74%.

However, it is essential to note that this model is part of a training project and has not been formally validated by scientific authorities. Consequently, the results should be interpreted with caution. The main limitations of this algorithm include:

  • The model relies on publicly available data, the quality of which could not be independently verified.
  • The ethnicity and geographic origin of the individuals in the dataset are unknown, even though these factors can significantly affect the prevalence of cardiovascular diseases.
  • The training data encompasses a limited age range (39 to 65 years). While the prevalence of cardiovascular disease increases with age, accuracy may diminish for individuals outside this range due to data extrapolation.
  • Only a limited set of characteristics were available in the training data. Notably, factors such as smoking status and gender did not influence the model, limiting the variables available for input.
  • Several features lacked detailed definitions, including smoking, alcohol consumption, and physical activity levels. Additionally, the thresholds for glucose and cholesterol levels were unspecified.

Despite these limitations, with just a few required inputs, this model can serve as a valuable tool, offering insights into one's cardiac health and encouraging users to seek further evaluation from healthcare professionals.
